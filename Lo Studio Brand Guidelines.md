# Lo Studio — Brand Guidelines

*A quick-reference brand sheet for decks, documents, and presentations.*

Lo Studio is the studio of **Michael DeBiase** — brand, web, product UI, motion, and go-to-market design for mission-driven startups and enterprises.

**The look in one line:** dark-first, editorial, and chrome-minimal — a near-black canvas, crisp Geist type, one electric "volt" yellow accent, and monospace labels for a technical, studio-grade feel.

## 1. Color

The system is a full dark theme. Contrast comes from subtle surface steps and the volt accent — not from flipping to light backgrounds.

### Core palette

| Swatch | Name | Hex | Use |
|--------|------|-----|-----|
| ⬛ | Background | `#141414` | Default slide / page background |
| ⬛ | Background deep | `#101010` | Deepest panels, full-bleed bands |
| ⬛ | Surface | `#1C1C1E` | Cards, raised panels |
| ⬛ | Surface high | `#242427` | Topmost cards, insets |
| ⬜ | Ink (white) | `#FFFFFF` | Primary text |
| 🟨 | **Volt** | `#D9F400` | **Primary accent — the single hero color** |
| 🟧 | Orange | `#FF3D00` | Secondary brand color, textures, selection |

### Text tints (white on dark)

| Level | Value | Use |
|-------|-------|-----|
| Primary | `#FFFFFF` | Headlines, key copy |
| Secondary | `rgba(255,255,255,.74)` | Sub-copy |
| Muted | `rgba(255,255,255,.60)` | Lead paragraphs, captions |
| Faint | `rgba(255,255,255,.40)` | Labels, numbers |
| Hairline | `rgba(255,255,255,.10)` | Borders, dividers |

### Accent support

| Name | Hex | Role |
|------|-----|------|
| Volt hover | `#E9FF45` | Brighter volt for hover / gradient top |
| Volt pressed | `#AEC700` | Darker volt for shadows / pressed |
| Volt ink | `#141400` | Text placed **on** a volt fill (never white) |
| Orange hover | `#FF7A4D` | Lighter orange |
| Orange dark | `#D12F00` | Selection highlight |
| Amber | `#FF9D33` | Warm tertiary |

### Color rules

- **Volt (`#D9F400`) is the only "loud" color.** Reserve it for one thing per view: a key stat, an active state, a CTA, an accent line. Don't flood large areas with it.
- **Text on volt is always near-black `#141400`** — never white.
- **Orange is a supporting color**, not a co-primary — use it for texture (dot grids), accents, and selection, not headlines.
- Keep backgrounds dark. If you must go light, this brand still resolves to a near-black canvas — avoid white slides.

## 2. Typography

Three families. All available on Google Fonts.

| Role | Family | Use |
|------|--------|-----|
| **Headline / body** | **Geist** | Titles, body copy, UI — weights 300–900 |
| **Labels / metadata** | **Geist Mono** | Eyebrows, section numbers, stat labels, captions — uppercase, wide tracking |
| **Emphasis** | **Instrument Serif** *(italic)* | One emphasis phrase, big quote marks, decorative flourish |

### Scale & treatment

| Style | Size | Weight | Notes |
|-------|------|--------|-------|
| Display | 48–116px | 800 | Tight tracking (-.05em), line-height ~0.92 |
| Section title | 32–60px | 700 | Tracking -.035em |
| Subhead | 26–38px | 700 | Tracking -.03em |
| Lead paragraph | 16–19px | 400 | Line-height 1.55, muted ink |
| Body | 15–16px | 400 | Line-height ~1.6 |
| Eyebrow / label | 12px | 500 | **Geist Mono**, UPPERCASE, .22em tracking |

**Conventions**
- Big headings get **tight negative tracking** and near-1.0 line-height for a dense, editorial feel.
- Anything that reads as **metadata** — eyebrows, numbers (`01`, `//`), stat labels, status pills — is **Geist Mono**, uppercase, wide-tracked.
- **Italic Instrument Serif is the only decorative voice.** Use it sparingly: one emphasis word, oversized quote marks.
- Use balanced wrapping on large headings and pull-quotes.

## 3. Logo

- **Primary wordmark** — "Lo Studio" in Geist, rendered **white on dark**.
- **Logo mark** — an articulated "walking figure" rig, solid white, with a soft glow behind the head. Used as favicon and badge.
- **Footer wordmark** — "Lo Studio" set massive and near-invisible (white at 3–16% opacity) as an embossed graphic.

**Logo rules**
- Keep clear space of at least the mark's own height around it.
- The mark is **monochrome white on dark** — never recolor it to volt or orange.
- Don't place the logo on a busy or light background without a dark backing.

---

## 4. Spacing, shape & texture

- **Content width:** cap reading width around 1180px; constrain body copy so lines don't run too long.
- **Generous vertical rhythm:** large breathing room between sections (~110px desktop).
- **Corner radii:** pills & buttons fully rounded (999px); cards 16px; large feature cards ~24px; small tiles 9–14px.
- **Borders:** hairlines only — 1px white at 10–16% opacity. Volt-tinted borders signal active / interactive.
- **Shadows:** large, soft, deep, dark — "floating glass." Volt elements add a colored glow.

**Signature textures** (use lightly for ambiance)
- **Grain** — faint fractal-noise overlay (~3.5%).
- **Dot grids** — subtle radial dot fields (orange on dark).
- **Volt bloom** — a blurred volt glow behind a hero element.

---

## 5. Components (visual cues to echo)

- **Primary button:** volt gradient fill, near-black text, soft volt glow; lifts slightly on hover.
- **Eyebrow + header:** mono uppercase kicker (often with a number) sitting above a large title and a muted lead.
- **Cards:** rounded media, mono labels, a circular arrow that flips to volt on hover.
- **Stats:** big rolling numerals with mono labels on a dark band.
- **Pills / filters:** mono text, hairline border; active = volt fill with near-black text.

When building slides, lean on: **mono eyebrow → big Geist headline → muted lead → one volt accent.** That rhythm reads as Lo Studio instantly.

---

## 6. Motion

- **Reveal on scroll:** content fades and rises ~26px, staggered.
- **Subtle hover lifts:** interactive surfaces rise 2–7px with deeper shadows.
- **Signature flourishes:** rotating role text, infinite marquee with volt asterisks, animated volt "border beam" on cards, rolling counters.
- Keep motion purposeful and quick. Respect reduced-motion: animations resolve to their visible end-state.

---

## 7. Voice & tone

Confident, lowercase-friendly, startup-fluent. Pair **plainspoken value statements** with **technical, monospace metadata** for a studio-grade, engineered tone.

**Example:**
> Michael DeBiase is a *brand designer.* The full creative stack for startups and enterprises. Providing effective design solutions that drive real growth.

**Do**
- Lead with outcomes ("drives real growth," "ship consistently").
- Keep it tight and human; let one italic word carry the emphasis.
- Use mono metadata (numbers, labels, statuses) to add a technical edge.

**Don't**
- Over-explain or pad with jargon.
- Use exclamation-heavy hype or emoji.
